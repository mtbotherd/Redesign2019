Minor service adjustments begin Saturday, June 13

As part of the ongoing, shared effort to limit the spread of COVID-19, Metro Transit will continue a reduced service schedule on Saturday, June 13. This is to address a sharp decline in ridership and to focus our resources where they are most needed to provide critical travel during this crisis.
Schedules that will go into effect on June 13 will keep service at about the same level it’s at today – roughly 65% what it was at the beginning of the year – with some minor adjustments to add peak-hour service. Light rail and Northstar schedules will remain as is. 

Continue to watch metrotransit.org/health for the latest information. 

Remember:

  • Use transit for essential travel only
  • Cover your nose and mouth with a fabric face covering while riding and at transit stops or stations.
  • Board and exit buses using the back door. The front doors and ramps are available for those who need it for any reason.
  • Keep distance between you and others, especially the driver.
  • When boarding, if the bus or train does not have space to allow adequate social distancing, the operator will display "Next Bus Please" on the overhead sign, notifying customers that they need to wait for the next trip. 
  • Metro Transit Service Centers and Lost & Found are closed. Transit Information is available by phone (612-373-3333) and text (612-444-1161) Monday-Friday 6:30 a.m. – 9 p.m.; Customer Relations is available by phone (612-373-3333) and online Monday-Friday 8 a.m. – 4:30 p.m.

Summary of Metro Transit service beginning Saturday, June 13:

  • On weekdays:
    • Most local bus routes will follow SATURDAY schedules
    • Some routes will follow Saturday schedules with trips added Monday through Friday
    • Commuter express routes will continue to operate at a reduced level
       
  • On all days of the week, many bus trips after 11 p.m. and before 4:30 a.m. have been restored on the following routes:
    2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 9, 10, 11, 14, 17, 18, 21, 22, 23, 54, 62, 63, 64, 65, 68, 71, 74, 87, 515, 612, 722, 724, METRO A Line, METRO C Line.
     
  • On all days of the week, light rail service will continue operation between 6 a.m. and 9 p.m. daily. 
    (METRO Blue Line airport shuttle between Terminal 1 and Terminal 2 stations continues to operate 24 hours-a-day.)
     

Sign up for Rider Alerts at metrotransit.org/alerts to learn about future changes to your specific routes via text or email.
